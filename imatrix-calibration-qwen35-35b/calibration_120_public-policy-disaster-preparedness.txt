# Resilient Community Framework: Multi-Hazard Preparedness Plan
**Scope:** Floods, Heatwaves, Prolonged Power Outages
**Version:** 1.0
**Target Audience:** Local Government, Neighborhood Associations, Homeowners, Businesses, and Residents.

---

## I. Mission & Guiding Principles
**Mission:** To build a resilient community capable of anticipating, absorbing, adapting to, and recovering from floods, extreme heat, and energy disruptions with minimal loss of life and property.

**Guiding Principles:**
1.  **Equity First:** Prioritize the elderly, disabled, low-income residents, and non-English speakers.
2.  **Neighbor-to-Neighbor:** Formalize mutual aid networks so reliance on outside resources is reduced for the first 72 hours.
3.  **Layered Defense:** Combine infrastructure hardening with behavioral change and early warning systems.

---

## II. Governance & Organization (The "Who")
Before a disaster strikes, a clear chain of command must be established to prevent chaos.

### 1. Community Resilience Task Force (CRTF)
*   **Coordinator:** One lead contact for city/state liaison.
*   **Hazard Lead (Flood):** Oversees drainage, sandbags, and evacuation routes.
*   **Hazard Lead (Heat):** Manages cooling centers and hydration points.
*   **Energy Lead:** Coordinates backup power generators and fuel distribution.
*   **Vulnerable Populations Officer:** Maintains the registry of those requiring assistance (medical needs, mobility issues).

### 2. Neighborhood Block Captains
*   Recruit one responsible volunteer per 10–20 households.
*   **Responsibilities:** Door-to-door welfare checks, distributing alerts to hard-to-reach homes, managing local resource caches.

---

## III. Cross-Cutting Infrastructure & Communication
These systems apply to all three hazard types.

### 1. Communication Tree
*   **Primary Channel:** Community app/messaging group (e.g., Nextdoor, Slack, Telegram).
*   **Secondary Channel:** SMS Broadcasts (for those without smartphones/internet).
*   **Tertiary Channel:** Battery-powered AM/FM Radios and NOAA Weather Radio stations.
*   **Low-Tech Backup:** Whistle codes or hand-written chalk signs for power-outage scenarios.

### 2. Community Resource Registry (CRR)
A digital and physical map of community assets:
*   **Generators:** Private vs. Public capacity and fuel types.
*   **Cooling Centers:** Locations with AC, medical support, and restrooms.
*   **Water Sources:** Wells, cisterns, or nearby public fountains safe for consumption.
*   **Vehicles:** List of residents willing to transport others to evacuation points (for floods).

### 3. Vulnerable Population Registry
*   Conducted annually during a "Resilience Month."
*   Identifies: Dialysis patients, oxygen users, seniors living alone, and mobility-impaired individuals.
*   **Protocol:** Block captains must conduct physical checks within the first 4 hours of any declared emergency.

## IV. Hazard-Specific Protocols

### A. Flood Preparedness & Response
**Goal:** Prevent water entry and evacuate safely if inundation occurs.

1.  **Pre-Season Mitigation:**
    *   Clear storm drains and ditches within neighborhood limits.
    *   Install flood barriers (levees or inflatable dams) at low-lying intersections.
    *   **Insurance Check:** Verify flood insurance coverage for all businesses and residents.
2.  **Response Action:**
    *   **Level 1 Warning:** Move vehicles to high ground; bring outdoor furniture in.
    *   **Level 2 Warning (Evacuation Order):** Follow designated elevation routes (avoid bridges and culverts). Do not drive through standing water ("Turn Around, Don't Drown").
    *   **Post-Flood Safety:** Assume water is contaminated. Avoid using tap water until "Boil Order" is lifted.

### B. Heatwave Preparedness & Response
**Goal:** Prevent heat exhaustion/stroke and manage energy load.

1.  **Pre-Season Mitigation:**
    *   **Tree Planting:** Strategic planting of shade trees along sidewalks to cool pedestrian zones by up to 5°C.
    *   **Cooling Center Mapping:** Identify libraries, community centers, and senior living facilities with backup generators.
2.  **Response Action:**
    *   **Heat Advisory:** Activate "Hydration Stations" at park entrances and bus stops (free ice/water).
    *   **Blackout + Heat:** If power fails during heat, open Cooling Centers immediately. Move vulnerable residents to shaded, ventilated basements or first floors.
    *   **Energy Saver Program:** Request voluntary reduction of AC use in large commercial buildings to prevent grid overload (via local utility partnership).

### C. Prolonged Power Outage Preparedness & Response
**Goal:** Maintain critical life support and minimize carbon monoxide risks.

1.  **Pre-Season Mitigation:**
    *   **Generator Safety Training:** Mandatory training for residents owning generators to prevent Carbon Monoxide poisoning (never operate indoors or in garages).
    *   **Fuel Storage:** Community fuel tank agreements with local gas stations for emergency diesel/petrol reserves.
2.  **Response Action:**
    *   **Grid Down Protocol:** Block Captains report status of streets and traffic signals to CRTF immediately.
    *   **Critical Care:** Electricity-dependent residents (CPAPs, oxygen) have a pre-identified evacuation plan to hospital generators or community backup hubs.
    *   **Food Safety:** Discard food if refrigerators warm above 40°F for >4 hours.

## V. The "Cascading Event" Scenario
*Often these hazards occur together (e.g., Flood destroys power lines; Heatwave occurs during Power Outage).*

**Unified Cascading Protocol:**
1.  **Priority 1:** Life Safety (Medical emergencies). Use battery backup for medical devices; move patients to cooling centers if AC is lost.
2.  **Priority 2:** Hygiene & Water (Water treatment plants need power, heatwaves dehydrate). Distribute water jugs and sanitation kits immediately.
3.  **Priority 3:** Security (Lack of lights/alarms). Organize "Community Watch" patrols with flashlights (not car headlights to save fuel) to prevent looting during evacuation or sheltering.

## VI. Training & Drills Schedule

| Frequency | Activity | Objective |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Quarterly** | **Tabletop Exercises** | CRTF tests communication channels and decision-making logic. |
| **Bi-Annually** | **Resident Drills** | "Power Outage Day": Residents practice 1-hour blackout at home; test siren/radio reception. |
| **Annually** | **Community Fair** | Free generator maintenance check, water purification demo, and first aid certification. |

---

## VII. Recovery & Review Phase (Post-Event)
*Disaster management does not end when the storm passes.*

1.  **After-Action Report (AAR):** Within 14 days of event conclusion, CRTF meets to identify gaps (e.g., "Generator fuel ran out," "Alerts didn't reach non-English speakers").
2.  **Damage Assessment:** Block Captains submit photos and checklists of damage (structural, flood debris, tree loss) for insurance/reimbursement processing.
3.  **Mental Health Support:** Organize community gatherings to discuss trauma; partner with local counseling services.
4.  **Framework Update:** Revise this document based on AAR findings.

---

## VIII. Implementation Checklist (Start Today)

- [ ] **Form the Task Force:** Identify 5 core members by Month 1.
- [ ] **Conduct Vulnerability Audit:** Contact neighbors over 65 or with disabilities (Month 2).
- [ ] **Build the Resource Map:** Locate all generators and cooling centers within a 2-mile radius (Month 3).
- [ ] **Distribute Emergency Kits:** Distribute "Go-Bags" for low-income households (Water, First Aid, Flashlight) by Month 6.
- [ ] **Test Communication:** Send a test alert via the primary channel to verify open rates.
