# Incident Disclosure & Communication Policy

**Policy Owner:** [Chief Information Security Officer / General Counsel]
**Effective Date:** [Date]
**Version:** 1.0
**Approved By:** [Board of Directors / Executive Leadership Team]

---

## 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish a structured, transparent, and compliant framework for communicating regarding cybersecurity incidents involving sensitive data or critical systems. This policy aims to:
*   Ensure timely notification to regulators, affected individuals, and partners in accordance with legal obligations.
*   Protect the organization's reputation through consistent messaging.
*   Minimize liability and maintain stakeholder trust.

## 2. Scope
This policy applies to all employees, contractors, temporary workers, and third-party vendors acting on behalf of **[Organization Name]**. It covers all systems, networks, devices, and data (on-premise, cloud, or hybrid) that store or process organizational information.

## 3. Governance: The Disclosure Committee
In the event of a **Significant Security Incident**, the **Incident Response Team (IRT)** must immediately convene a **Cross-Functional Disclosure Committee**.

**Committee Composition:**
*   **Executive Sponsor:** [CEO / COO] (Final decision authority on public messaging).
*   **Legal & Compliance:** [General Counsel / CCO] (Determines legal notification obligations and timing).
*   **Security Lead:** [CISO / Director of InfoSec] (Provides technical facts, root cause, and remediation status).
*   **Communications Lead:** [Head of PR / Corporate Comms] (Drafts public statements and manages media inquiries).
*   **HR Lead:** If employee data is involved.
*   **Customer Success/Account Management:** If client impact is identified.

**Authority:** The **Legal & Compliance Officer** holds veto power regarding the timing of regulatory notifications. The **Executive Sponsor** holds veto power regarding public press statements.

## 4. Incident Classification & Disclosure Triggers
Disclosure decisions are based on the severity of the incident and the type of data compromised.

| Classification | Definition | Disclosure Requirement |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Critical** | PII, PHI, financial data, or intellectual property breached; System disruption > 24 hrs. | Mandatory Notification (Regulatory & Affected Users). |
| **High** | Credential theft, internal network access without data exfiltration. | Internal Review; External Notification if risk to clients is probable. |
| **Low** | Isolated attempt, blocked, no data access. | Internal Log Only; No external disclosure required. |

## 5. Notification Timelines & Obligations
Timelines are based on standard regulatory requirements (e.g., GDPR Art 33/34, NYDFS 500, SEC Rules). **[Organization Name]** must verify these against its specific operating jurisdictions.

*   **Regulatory Bodies:** Notified within [24 / 72 / 4] hours of confirmation that a breach requires reporting.
*   **Affected Individuals (Customers):** Contacted without unreasonable delay (typically within 30 days), with guidance to mitigate potential harm.
*   **Credit Bureaus / Financial Regulators:** Notified according to specific industry mandates (e.g., NCUA, OCC).
*   **SEC (Public Companies):** If the incident is "material," a Form 8-K may be required within [4] business days.

## 6. Communication Guidelines

### 6.1. The "One Voice" Protocol
To prevent rumors and misinformation, all external inquiries must be directed solely to the Corporate Communications Department.
*   **Employees:** Are strictly prohibited from posting on social media (LinkedIn, X/Twitter, etc.) regarding the incident until an official statement is released.
*   **Media:** All media inquiries are routed to [Contact Email/Phone].

### 6.2. Content Standards
All notifications must adhere to the following principles:
*   **Accuracy:** Do not speculate on the timeline or scope of the attack before it is confirmed by Security.
*   **Transparency:** Acknowledge what happened clearly without minimizing the impact.
*   **Action-Oriented:** Focus on steps taken and steps users can take (e.g., "Change password," "Monitor accounts").
*   **Support:** Provide a dedicated toll-free number and web page for support inquiries.

### 6.3. Notification Template Structure
External notifications to individuals must include:
1.  Date of discovery and estimated timeframe of the incident.
2.  Types of data involved (e.g., Name, Email, SSN).
3.  Action taken by **[Organization Name]** to secure systems.
4.  Steps the individual should take to protect themselves.
5.  Contact information for **[Organization Name]** and credit monitoring services provided.

## 7. Third-Party & Partner Notification
*   **Vendors:** If the breach originated from a vendor or impacts their data, they must be notified immediately per Master Service Agreements (MSA).
*   **Business Partners:** Notify key business partners directly if the incident disrupts service delivery to them.

## 8. Post-Incident Review
Following the resolution of the incident and all mandatory notifications, the Disclosure Committee shall conduct a "Post-Mortem" within [30] days.
*   **Evaluate:** Was the timeline met? Did the messaging cause unnecessary panic? Were regulators satisfied with our cooperation?
*   **Update:** Modify this policy based on lessons learned and changes in legislation.

## 9. Training & Drills
This policy will be integrated into the Incident Response Plan. Tabletop exercises involving Legal, Communications, and Security must be conducted at least **[Annually]** to test notification workflows and timelines.

## 10. Compliance Statement
Failure to adhere to this policy may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination, as well as legal liability for [Organization Name] and the individual involved.
