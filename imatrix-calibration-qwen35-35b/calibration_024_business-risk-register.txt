Below is a comprehensive **Risk Register** designed for a company migrating critical services to cloud infrastructure. This document follows industry best practices (based on ISO 31000 and NIST frameworks) to ensure clarity, accountability, and actionable mitigation.

### Risk Register: Critical Cloud Migration Project

**Project Overview:** Migration of core business systems (e.g., ERP, CRM, Customer Database) from on-premise data centers to a public/private cloud environment.
**Risk Scoring Methodology:**
*   **Likelihood:** 1 (Rare) to 5 (Almost Certain)
*   **Impact:** 1 (Insignificant) to 5 (Catastrophic)
*   **Risk Score:** Likelihood × Impact (Range: 1–25)
    *   *Low:* 1–6 | *Medium:* 7–14 | *High:* 15–20 | *Critical:* 21–25

### Implementation Guidance

#### 1. Risk Treatment Options
In addition to **Mitigation** (reducing likelihood or impact), the Risk Owner must consider these alternative approaches:
*   **Avoid:** Canceling the migration of specific high-risk modules that do not add value.
*   **Transfer:** Purchasing cyber insurance or outsourcing specific security tasks to a specialized third party.
*   **Accept:** Acknowledging low-priority risks where the cost of mitigation exceeds the potential loss.

#### 2. Monitoring & Reporting
*   **Review Cadence:** This register must be reviewed bi-weekly during active migration phases and monthly thereafter.
*   **Trigger Events:** New risks should be added immediately if a critical vendor change, budget shift, or threat landscape update occurs (e.g., new ransomware tactics).
*   **Residual Risk:** Post-mitigation assessment. Even with controls in place, what is the remaining risk level? If "High," executive sign-off is required to proceed.

#### 3. Critical Success Factors
To ensure this register remains effective rather than just a paperwork exercise:
*   **Communication:** Ensure all stakeholders (Finance, Legal, IT) understand their role regarding specific risks.
*   **Automation:** Where possible, automate risk checks (e.g., automated compliance scanning within the CI/CD pipeline) rather than relying on manual audits.
*   **Transparency:** High-risk items must be visible to Executive Leadership; hiding risks creates a false sense of security during the cutover.
