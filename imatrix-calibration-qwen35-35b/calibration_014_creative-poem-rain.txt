The sky hangs low, a bruise of heavy lead,
Above the canyon where the concrete breathes.
Steam rises slow from grates beneath the feet,
And puddles hold the fractured neon dreams.

The air is sharp with ozone and wet metal,
Where waterfalls cascade off gutter rims.
Cold mist clings close like lace against the skin,
While distant sirens hum a muffled hymn.

The scent of wet asphalt fills the lungs,
Mixed with coffee steam from corner stalls.
A pink light bleeds through windows in the east,
To wash the shadows off the sleeping walls.

Now silence breaks beneath the traffic's roar,
And raindrops fade where morning lights return.
