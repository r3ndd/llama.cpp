[SCENE START]

**INT. KITCHEN - NIGHT**

Heavy rain Hammers against the windows of a modest, two-story house. The power FLICKERS and dies, plunging the room into darkness save for the faint, strobe-like glow of lightning outside.

LEO (32), dressed in business casual with a tie loosened around his neck, stands by the counter. He holds a single phone with its flashlight ON.

MAYA (29) sits at the small round table, her arms wrapped around herself, staring into a cold cup of coffee.

A THUNDERCLAP rattles the window frame. Leo flinches. Maya doesn't blink.

LEO
It's not just about the numbers anymore. The roof needs replacing before winter. The HVAC is seven years out.

MAYA
(Without looking up)
I can fix the roof. I learned how from Dad.

Leo turns on his phone light, sweeping it over the room. It passes over a row of empty wall hooks.

LEO
You're going to be busy saving the house if you're working forty hours at the clinic and fixing gutters. That's the math, Maya.

MAYA
That's your math.

The power STUTTERS back on for a second, then dies again. In the sudden dark, their voices sound closer.

LEO
(Darker)
It's not about *my* need to move, it's about this place dying. Look around. We're living in a mausoleum. I'm trying to keep us from drowning in maintenance bills while we pretend we're fine here.

Maya finally looks up. Her eyes are red-rimmed, but her chin is set.

MAYA
This isn't about the bills. It's about where he planted his garden. Where she painted the kitchen. This house... it remembers us. If we sell it, we burn that memory. What happens if one of us needs to come back? There won't be a place.

LEO
There will always be hotels, Maya. Or we stay in the same state and buy something newer. Something with a garage that actually closes.

MAYA
(Gestures at the ceiling)
Do you remember the leak in the hallway? When I was twelve? The water came down right above my bed.

LEO
I remember.

MAYA
You stayed up all night reading to me so I wouldn't be scared of the sound of dripping. You taped it off with plastic. We spent a whole summer waiting for that rain on that roof. That's why I can't sell it. It's not a real estate asset. It's... proof we survived the bad weather.

Leo lowers his phone. The beam points at the floor.

LEO
(Quietly)
I'm scared, Maya. Not of the house. But that I'm going to leave without looking back. I feel like if this sells, it means it's really over. That we're just... strangers now.

MAYA
(Softening)
We've always been the ones who fix things. We can fix this one last time. But only together.

Leo steps around the table, the flashlight beam catching a dust motes dancing in the air. He looks at his sister, then back at the rain-streaked window.

LEO
If we keep it... you have to help me with the roof inspections next week. And I'm not paying a single penny more for insurance unless you move that junk from the attic.

A beat. A low rumble of thunder shakes the house. Maya lets out a breath she didn't know she was holding.

MAYA
Okay. Deal. But no more business talk until the lights stay on for five minutes straight.

Leo pockets his phone. He pulls out the chair opposite her and sits down. The kitchen is dim, but they are both in it now.

LEO
Deal.

Another flash of lightning illuminates the room. They sit together in the dark, listening to the rain.

**FADE OUT.**
