Below is a draft for a **Basic AI Governance Policy** tailored for a mid-size company using Large Language Models (LLMs) in Customer Support. It balances efficiency with risk management, focusing on security, accuracy, and accountability without creating excessive bureaucracy.

***

# 🛡️ Internal AI Governance Policy: Customer Support LLMs

**Version:** 1.0
**Effective Date:** [Date]
**Owner:** Head of Customer Experience / CTO
**Applicability:** All employees, contractors, and vendors interacting with or developing tools involving Generative AI for customer support operations.

---

## 1. Purpose & Objective
To establish a framework for the safe, ethical, and effective use of Generative AI (LLMs) in our Customer Support ecosystem. This policy aims to protect customer data, maintain brand integrity, ensure regulatory compliance, and manage the risk of hallucinations or misinformation while leveraging AI to improve response times.

## 2. Scope
This policy applies to:
*   **Tools:** Public LLMs (e.g., ChatGPT, Claude), Enterprise AI Models, and AI plugins within our CRM/Helpdesk software.
*   **Users:** All support agents, quality assurance teams, engineering staff managing these tools, and external vendors implementing AI solutions.
*   **Data:** All customer interactions, PII (Personally Identifiable Information), and proprietary company data processed during support workflows.

## 3. Core Principles
All use of AI in Customer Support must adhere to these four pillars:
1.  **Privacy First:** No sensitive customer data is sent to non-secure models or used for public model training without explicit consent.
2.  **Human Oversight:** AI is an assistant, not a replacement. Critical decisions require human verification.
3.  **Transparency:** Customers are informed when they are interacting with automation.
4.  **Accountability:** Humans retain final responsibility for all outputs and communications.

## 4. Data Security & Privacy Protocols
### 4.1 Data Classification
*   **Restricted Data:** PII (names, emails, phone numbers), payment info, passwords, and medical/health data.
*   **Public/Internal Data:** Product manuals, public FAQs, internal policies.

### 4.2 Usage Rules
*   **Enterprise Model Requirement:** All LLMs processing customer support tickets must use the company's enterprise-tier provider with "Data Privacy" settings enabled (disabling model training on inputs).
*   **PII Redaction:** Support agents and AI systems must scrub PII before passing it to an LLM unless strictly necessary for the context, in which case a secure instance must be used.
*   **Public Tools Ban:** Agents are prohibited from pasting customer data, error logs containing credentials, or proprietary code into public-facing chat interfaces (e.g., free versions of ChatGPT).

## 5. Operational Guidelines for Support Agents
### 5.1 AI Outputs
*   **No Hallucinations:** Agents must verify facts provided by the AI before sending them to a customer. Do not use AI to fabricate policies or product features.
*   **Prompt Hygiene:** Agents should avoid sharing specific customer IDs in prompts unless necessary for the tool's function.
*   **Disclaimer:** Automated responses generated by AI must include a subtle indicator (e.g., "AI Assistant" or similar, depending on current compliance standards) if the interaction is fully automated.

### 5.2 Human Escalation (Fallback)
*   AI should flag issues requiring human attention based on sentiment analysis (anger/frustration), complexity, or specific keywords (e.g., "refund," "legal," "threat").
*   **Rule:** If a customer explicitly asks for a human, the AI must transfer immediately without attempting to persuade.

### 5.3 Customer Trust
*   We do not use AI to impersonate humans in ways that deceive customers regarding the nature of the assistance they are receiving (aligned with FTC guidelines).

## 6. Roles & Responsibilities
| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **AI Steering Committee** (Cross-functional) | Approve new AI vendors, review policy exceptions, oversee risk assessments. |
| **Support Manager** | Monitor AI performance, ensure agents follow protocols, approve escalation flows. |
| **Legal & Compliance** | Review data usage against GDPR/CCPA/local laws; assess liability risks. |
| **Security Team** | Ensure vendor connections (APIs) are secure and data is encrypted in transit/storage. |
| **Support Agents** | Follow prompt guidelines, verify AI answers, escalate confidently. |

## 7. Monitoring & Auditing
### 7.1 Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
We will track the following monthly to detect drift or issues:
*   **Escalation Rate:** % of conversations moved from AI to Human. (High rate = accuracy issues).
*   **Customer Satisfaction (CSAT):** Score comparison between AI and Human-assisted tickets.
*   **Hallucination Reports:** Number of confirmed factual errors introduced by AI.

### 7.2 Random Audits
The Quality Assurance team will randomly audit 5% of AI-generated responses monthly for accuracy, tone, and compliance with safety guidelines.

## 8. Incident Response
In the event of an AI failure (e.g., generating harmful content, leaking PII, or giving incorrect financial advice):
1.  **Stop:** Pause the specific feature or model immediately via a centralized admin console.
2.  **Notify:** Alert the Support Manager and CTO within 1 hour of discovery.
3.  **Contain:** Identify affected customers and notify them if data was compromised (per privacy policy).
4.  **Root Cause Analysis:** Determine if the issue was the model, the prompt engineering, or the agent's misuse.
5.  **Remediate:** Update system guardrails or retrain prompts before restarting.

## 9. Training & Onboarding
*   All support staff must complete an **"AI Safety for Agents"** workshop prior to using LLM tools.
*   Annual refreshers on prompt engineering, privacy risks, and ethical usage.

## 10. Policy Review
This policy will be reviewed **quarterly** or whenever a significant incident occurs. It is subject to change as AI regulations evolve (e.g., EU AI Act, US Executive Orders).

***

### 🚀 Implementation Checklist for Leadership
*   [ ] **Vendor Check:** Confirm that your LLM provider has an enterprise agreement that forbids data training on client inputs.
*   [ ] **Technical Guardrails:** Implement input/output filtering to block PII from entering the chat window (if not handled by the vendor).
*   [ ] **Human Handoff:** Configure the CRM to trigger a "High Priority" tag when sentiment analysis detects customer anger.
*   [ ] **Training Deck:** Create a one-page guide on "Top 5 Prompts to Avoid" for your agents.
