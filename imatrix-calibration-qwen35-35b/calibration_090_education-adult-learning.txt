This workshop plan is designed around the principles of **Andragogy** (adult learning), emphasizing self-direction, practical application, and peer-to-peer networking. It focuses on moving from "consuming" data to "creating" value with data.

### **Workshop Title:** From Data Curiosity to Career Confidence
**Subtitle:** A 4-Day Intensive for Career Transitioners in the Data Field

---

### **1. Executive Summary**
*   **Target Audience:** Adults (25+) pivoting from non-tech sectors (e.g., marketing, admin, retail) into data roles (Analyst, Business Intelligence, Operations Research).
*   **Duration:** 4 Full Days (6 hours/day) + Pre/Post-work modules.
*   **Format:** Hybrid or In-Person (Virtual is acceptable with breakout rooms).
*   **Class Size:** Max 20 learners for effective mentorship.
*   **Prerequisites:** Basic computer literacy, willingness to learn logic over memorization, access to a laptop with internet.

---

### **2. Learning Objectives**
By the end of this workshop, participants will be able to:
1.  **Define** the modern data landscape and specific career paths.
2.  **Query** real-world databases using SQL to extract actionable insights.
3.  **Clean and Analyze** datasets using Python (Pandas) or Excel/Google Sheets.
4.  **Visualize** data effectively to tell a business story (Tableau/PowerBI).
5.  **Portfolio** their work into a GitHub repository or website.
6.  **Articulate** their value proposition in resumes and interviews.

---

### **3. Pre-Workshop Phase (1 Week Before)**
*Adult learners often fail to finish workshops because they lack "foundational housekeeping" first.*

*   **Week -7 Days:** Skill Assessment Survey (Math background, coding experience).
*   **Week -4 Days:** Environment Setup Guide (Install VS Code, Anaconda, SQL client, or set up Google Colab accounts).
*   **Assignment:** Participants bring one dataset from their previous career field (e.g., Sales logs, HR records) for the "Capstone Project."

---

### **4. The 4-Day Curriculum**

#### **Day 1: Data Literacy & The Business Context**
*Goal: Demystify data and understand "Why" we use it.*
*   **09:00 – 10:30:** **Icebreaker & Mindset:** Imposter syndrome, the growth mindset, and defining roles (Analyst vs. Engineer vs. Scientist).
*   **10:45 – 12:30:** **Data Lifecycle Workshop:** ETL basics, Data Governance, Ethics, and Privacy (GDPR/CCPA awareness).
*   **13:30 – 15:00:** **Excel/Sheets Advanced:** Pivot tables, VLOOKUP/XLOOKUP as a foundation for logical thinking.
*   **15:15 – 16:30:** **Group Activity:** Analyzing the pre-work dataset to find 3 anomalies or trends (no coding required yet).
*   **Key Takeaway:** Understanding that data is a tool for decision-making, not just math.

#### **Day 2: The Engine Room (SQL)**
*Goal: Mastering the most requested skill in entry-level data.*
*   **09:00 – 11:00:** **Database Foundations:** Relational databases, Schema, Keys, and normalization concepts.
*   **11:15 – 13:00:** **SQL Syntax (Interactive):** SELECT, WHERE, GROUP BY, JOINs.
    *   *Activity:* "The Mystery Shopper" – Solve business questions by querying a sample retail database.
*   **14:00 – 16:00:** **Advanced SQL & Optimization:** Window Functions (RANK, ROW_NUMBER) and Subqueries.
*   **16:00 – 17:00:** **Debugging Clinic:** Learners share the errors they hit; group troubleshooting session.
*   **Key Takeaway:** Ability to extract specific data points needed for analysis without exporting to Excel.

#### **Day 3: Analysis & Visualization (Python + BI)**
*Goal: Moving from extraction to interpretation.*
*   **09:00 – 12:00:** **Intro to Python for Data:**
    *   Jupyter Notebooks, Variables, Lists, and the Pandas Library.
    *   Handling missing data (Imputation vs. Dropping).
*   **13:00 – 15:00:** **Statistical Basics for Business:** Mean/Median, Standard Deviation, Correlation vs. Causation.
*   **15:15 – 17:00:** **Visualization Storytelling:** Choosing the right chart (Bar vs. Line vs. Scatter). Introduction to PowerBI or Tableau Public.
*   **Key Takeaway:** Transforming raw numbers into a visual narrative that non-technical stakeholders understand.

#### **Day 4: Career Transition & Portfolio Building**
*Goal: Selling their new skills to employers.*
*   **09:00 – 11:30:** **The Capstone Build:** Participants work on transforming their pre-work dataset into a 5-slide executive summary + code notebook.
*   **12:30 – 14:00:** **Resume & LinkedIn Overhaul:** "Translating" previous experience (e.g., "Managed inventory" becomes "Managed supply chain logistics data").
*   **14:00 – 16:00:** **Mock Interviews:** Behavioral questions + SQL coding challenges (live).
    *   *Focus:* STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result) and explaining technical concepts simply.
*   **16:00 – 17:00:** **Networking & Roadmap:** Setting up GitHub, joining communities (DataTalks.Club, Kaggle), and next steps for self-study.

---

### **5. The Capstone Project Structure**
To ensure learning retention, the workshop culminates in a mini-project.
*   **The Brief:** Participants use their own dataset OR a provided real-world dataset (e.g., Spotify trends, Airbnb listings).
*   **The Deliverables:**
    1.  A SQL script to extract/prepare data.
    2.  A Python Notebook with cleaning and analysis code.
    3.  A PDF Dashboard or Slide Deck summarizing the business insight.
    4.  A GitHub README explaining how to run the code.

---

### **6. Facilitation Strategy for Adult Learners**
*   **Peer Mentoring:** Assign "Buddy Pairs" so learners aren't reliant solely on instructors. Adults learn well by teaching others.
*   **Flexible Pacing:** Provide a "Fast Track" for those who know SQL already, and an "Anchor Guide" for those needing more math support.
*   **Real-World Scenarios:** Never use abstract numbers (e.g., "x = 5"). Use scenarios (e.g., "Revenue dropped by 10% in Q3... why?").
*   **Breakout Rooms:** Adults can be introverted; structured breakout tasks reduce pressure during group work.

---

### **7. Post-Workshop Support (The 8-Week Bridge)**
*Completion does not guarantee employment; community does.*
*   **Week 1-2:** Weekly 30-minute Q&A calls for troubleshooting code.
*   **Week 4:** Guest speaker from HR/Tech Recruiter on what they look for in portfolios.
*   **Week 6:** Portfolio review session (peer-to-peer feedback).
*   **Ongoing:** Access to a private Discord/Slack channel for job postings and networking.

---

### **8. Resource & Tech Requirements**
*   **Hardware:** Participants need their own laptops with at least 8GB RAM.
*   **Software:**
    *   Anaconda Distribution (Python)
    *   PostgreSQL / Docker (or cloud-based SQL like BigQuery Sandbox)
    *   VS Code or Jupyter Lab
    *   PowerBI Desktop / Tableau Public
*   **Data Repositories:** Kaggle, Google Dataset Search, GitHub "Awesome Datasets."

### **9. Success Metrics (KPIs)**
1.  **Completion Rate:** >85% of attendees finish the Capstone Project.
2.  **Satisfaction Score:** Net Promoter Score (NPS) >70 from learners.
3.  **Job Readiness:** Post-workshop survey: "Do you feel confident applying for junior roles?"
4.  **Placement Rate:** Tracking how many secure an interview or role within 90 days (optional long-term metric).

---

### **10. Risk Management**
*   **Risk:** Technical setup takes too long on Day 1.
    *   *Mitigation:* Provide pre-loaded cloud environments (e.g., Google Colab, DataCamp Workspace) so no installation is required on laptops.
*   **Risk:** Low math anxiety causing dropout.
    *   *Mitigation:* Frame statistics as "tools" rather than "calculus." Emphasize Python libraries do the heavy lifting; focus on *interpretation* of results.
*   **Risk:** Learners get overwhelmed by scope.
    *   *Mitigation:* Strict time-boxing. Focus is on *Generalist Data Analyst*, not Specialist Engineer or Data Scientist, to keep the career goal attainable.
